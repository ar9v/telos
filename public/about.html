About Telos

Need

People, especially students, tend to procrastinate not necessarily out of laziness but rather due to things such as analysis paralysis . Even though analysis paralysis can be caused by a myriad of things, one very identifiable and controllable cause is usually anxiety due to poor time management. Furthermore, poor time management isn't necessarily due to lack of eort, but sometimes even due to the contrary: overexertion in a particular task. Countless time management methodologies have been created, but one of the simplest is the Pomodoro Technique . The Pomodoro Technique mitigates the problem of overexertion by limiting working bursts to twenty-five minutes at a time. Overall, we believe it is a very useful technique. However, what happens when you dedicate more bursts (pomodoros) to a specific task? We believe that there is a need to further refine the technique based on how courses are set up (e.g. each course is 8 units, so 5 hours out of class for work). In general, we think that there is a need for a simple, Pomodoro-based, set of tools that provides comprehensive means to time management without being too overwhelming (i.e. something neither too professional nor too basic).
